DEFENSE QUESTIONS WITH ANSWERS

1. What is the role of the PHP system?
PHP serves as the Hospital Management System for admin users.

2. What is the role of the C# system?
C# serves as the Billing and Invoice System.

3. What database is used?
SQLite is used.

4. How are the systems connected?
The C# system connects to PHP through api.php.

5. Does C# directly connect to SQLite?
No. C# connects through the PHP API.

6. What records can PHP manage?
Patients, doctors, appointments, admissions, services, and invoice monitoring.

7. What records can C# manage?
Invoices, invoice items, and payment updates.

8. What happens when C# saves an invoice?
The PHP API stores it in SQLite and the PHP admin can view it.

9. What is the purpose of hospital services?
They are predefined charges that can be added to invoices.

10. Why use SQLite?
It is simple, local, and does not require MySQL setup.

11. What is the default hospital admin account?
admin@hospital.test / admin123

12. What is the default billing account?
billing@hospital.test / billing123

13. What is subtotal?
Subtotal is the total amount before discount and tax.

14. What is balance?
Balance is total amount minus amount paid.

15. What payment statuses are used?
Unpaid, Partial, and Paid.

16. What is the API URL?
http://localhost:8000/api.php

17. Why is login separated by role?
Admin uses the PHP hospital system while billing staff uses the C# billing system.

18. What makes the systems connected?
They share the same SQLite database through the PHP API.

19. What can improve this system?
Receipt printing, insurance claims, official reports, and role permissions.

20. What is the main purpose of the pair?
To connect hospital operations with billing and invoice processing.
